Reviewing our lesson concepts

This worksheet walks you through the 208 concepts we use to tag and find lessons — things like plant parts, measurement, or figurative language. You'll go through them one at a time.

For each concept you decide what happens to it: keep it, fold it into a similar concept, add a new one, or remove it if it doesn't belong. Those decisions give the team a clean, trusted list to tag lessons against.

You don't have to read every note. We've suggested an answer wherever we could — your job is to confirm it or change it.

Three kinds of decisions

  • Quick check — Most concepts come with a suggested answer. Glance at it and either agree or pick something different.
  • Your call — A handful come with no suggestion. These are the ones worth a careful look — you decide from a blank slate.
  • Group decision — A few small groups of closely-related concepts are decided together: you make one choice for the group and it carries down to each concept in it.

The worksheet sorts every concept into the right kind for you — you'll see a small label on each one. The counts at the top tell you how many of each are left.

Saving and sharing your work

Your decisions save to this browser automatically as you go — the Saved locally badge in the top corner confirms it. You can close the tab and come back later.

When you're done — or want to hand off — open the ⚙ Advanced menu (top right) and choose Save & Export markdown ↓. That gives you a file you can send to the team. To resume from a saved file, use Import a saved markdown file… in the same menu.

That's everything. Ready when you are.
